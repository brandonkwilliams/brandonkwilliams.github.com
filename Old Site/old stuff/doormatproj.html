Antimicrobial Doormat

Fall Qtr. 2012/Winter Qtr. 2013
DSGN 298/398
Non-Sequitur Solutions

Research/Observation

Define Requirements

  • We researched existing solutions and talked to an expert to gain knowledge in the field of microbiology
  • We observed and interviewed users in their home to understand what their current habits are and look for opportunities
  • Based on the user observations, we determined that the most critical requirements were that the mat be non-slip, thin, modular, easy to clean, and better at cleaning and drying shoes upon entry

Design Ideation

Mockup/Prototype

  • We generated ideas through brainstorming with little restriction on form or function
  • We then decided on a multi-material mat surface composition paired with a modular tile approach
  • Finally, after testing several iterations of our design, we arrived at a final design
  • We built many mockups along the way to test new ideas as they came up
  • This particular mockup allowed us to quantify the absorbent ability of the mop yarn and also set us up for building of our final prototype
  • I built this mockup using a piece of perforated plastic sheet by hooking loops of the mop yarn through the perforations
