Guitar Case

Spring/Summer 2012
All-State Industries Inc.

Brainstorming & Research

Initial Design

  • First, I researched existing products and the client's provided images to determine their taste and design ideas.
  • Then, I talked to the clients to determine their wants and specifications.
  • I then created brainstorm sketches based on the information I received from the clients.
  • I used the sketches to create SolidWorks models of three design ideas.
  • I then Shared these models with the clients to receive feedback and determine design direction.
  • Next, I gathered design features from the client to be incorporated in the final design.

Design for Prototyping

Final Prototype

  • I created engineering SolidWorks models for manufacturing processes: vacuum-formed plastic shells, CNC machined foam inserts, and rapid-prototyped Tuning Station accessory.
  • K2 Plastics Inc. manufactured and assembled a final prototype using the pieces that had been designed.
  • It was rewarding to see the product through the design process from an idea to a physical prototype.

Antimicrobial Doormat

Fall Qtr. 2012/Winter Qtr. 2013
DSGN 298/398 & Non-Sequitur Solutions

Research & User Observation

Key Requirements

  • We researched existing solutions and talked to an expert to gain knowledge in the field of microbiology.
  • We conducted simple bacterial testing to better understand the effectiveness of different green antimicrobials.
  • We also observed and interviewed users in their homes to better understand what their current habits are and look for opportunities.
  • Based on the user observations, we determined that the most critical requirements were that the mat be non-slip, thin, modular, easy to clean, and better at cleaning and drying shoes upon entry.

Mockup & Testing

Final Prototype

  • I built this mockup from mop yarn and perforated plastic sheet by hooking loops of the mop yarn through the perforations.
  • This mockup allowed us to test the absorbency of the mop yarn in comparison to other competitive samples.
  • We created our final prototype by 3D printing a flexible rubber material for the scraping elements and then interweaved mop yarn in a similar fashion to our mockup.
  • The design also features a connection scheme that gives the mat system modularity within a user's space.
