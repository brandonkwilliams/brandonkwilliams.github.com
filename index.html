about

My name is Brandon Williams and I am a hybrid Engineer/Designer. I fell in love with designing and building things at a young age. Growing up on a farm and around a manufacturing plant, I became fascinated with how things worked and were made.

This passion of building and manufacturing led me to Northwestern University where I received a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering in 2013. While studying engineering, I discovered that my favorite classes all involved the human-centered side of design and engineering.

I am now currently pursuing a Master's of Science in Engineering Design Innovation at Northwestern University. EDI pairs my technical background with a Human-Centered Design focus.

This combination allows me to design with the end in mind, understanding how design decisions affect manufacturing.

process

RESEARCH

Every project begins with a problem. Whether or not the stated problem is the right problem is often an interesting challenge. It takes time and effort to truly understand the actual needs of the user.

SYNTHESIS

This is one of, if not the, most important steps of the process. By taking needs found in observations and distilling them, we are able to turn them into useful directions for exploration and design.

IDEATION

This is the beginning of the fun for me. Starting to bring ideas out of my head and onto paper is an extremely rewarding task. I love to sketch and think through ideas visually beginning to develop form and function simultaneously.

PROTOTYPING

Prototyping is another of my favorite parts of the process. Building physical representations of ideas requires a wide variety of skills and I am always eager to learn any skill that has potential value in prototyping.

TESTING

An idea is just that until it is validated. Testing ideas constantly with users to gain feedback is key if the end product hopes to be successful. It is best to test ideas early and often to gain as much feedback as possible.

ITERATION

Test results should circle the process back to the beginning. Iteration involves redefining needs, continuing ideation, constant prototyping, and additional testing. This feedback loop allows me to evolve ideas until they are fully grown.

ENGINEERING

I've never been satisfied with only rough prototypes of potential products. To me, there is something satisfying in the ability to bring the actual product to life. Although not all of my work gets manufactured, I always try to design products that have the potential to be made.
